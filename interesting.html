About Me

I am Ph.d. candidate in the Technologies in Practice research group at the IT University of Copenhagen. My project explores how individuals from diverse backgrounds assist each other in navigating mandatory digital self-services and infrastructures in Denmark to access government benefits or communicate with the public authorities.

Collective access in welfare provision

I study different forms of collective access in digitalized versions of welfare provision. Collective forms of access emerge in relations of solidarity and mutual respect. In the context of welfare provision, I observe a kind of collective access politics that emerges as a response to inaccessible digital services and power assymetries between citizens and the state. My research is intended to inform digital policies and design approaches in the Nordic region so that the digitalization of welfare provision benefits minoritized and marginalized populations.

This project is financed by Nordforsk as a part of the Nordic research collaboration: Infrastructures for Partially Digital Citizens: Supporting Informal Welfare Work in the Digitized State.

Wait, what is an alt-narrative?

If you're new to disability culture, you may be interested in learning more about image descriptions. Alt-text, or alternative text, is a written description, often in the form of metadata, that enables blind, low vision, and neurodivergent folks, who use screen readers, to access and enjoy digital images. Check out the project Alt-Text as Poetry or the paper Exploring AltNarrative in HCI Imagery and Comics by Makayla Lewis and colleagues (2022) to learn more about it. Typically, alt-text is hidden visually as metadata. On this site I write alt-text metadata and longer alt-narratives as captions to raise awareness about alt-text's narrative potential.
