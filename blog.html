Visual Ethnography: While We Wait for Time to Pass (2020)

A photography project that explores Malapascua, an island in the Philippines that is 2.7 km long and 1 km wide, located north of Cebu province. The island is well-known for its diving sites that allow people to swim with thresher sharks throughout most of the year. However, during the pandemic, stories about the island's inhabitants remained untold. The project began when the World Health Organization announced the pandemic on March 11, 2020, interrupting my journey across the Philippines. These series of portraits show different stages of the lockdown, from General Community Quarantine (GCQ) to Enhanced Community Quarantine (ECQ). Each portrait is accompanied by a short text that provides context to the stories shared with me by the island's residents during my stay, which took place between March 2 and May 25, 2020.

Support Malapascua

If you wish to help islanders, here are some programs started by residents on Malapascua that you can support:

  1. Back to School Malapascua: support the children of Malapascua to go back to school during the pandemic.
  2. Villa Sandra feeding program: every Saturday, Villa Sandra Guest House provides meals to children in need accompanied by art and crafts learning activities.
  3. Malapascua Paw Heroes: fund the vaccination, neutering and care of stray animals in Malapascua.
  4. People and the Sea: protect the local marine life.

Videos for Fotografisk Center(2017-2020)

Over the course of three years I filmed, edited, and produced short interviews with visual artists for Fotografisk Center, a gallery showing camera-based art and new media. You can find some of my productions on Fotografisk Center's YouTube Channel.
