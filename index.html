User Research and Digital Accessibility

User Research

As an experienced user researcher committed to disability access, I identify diverse user needs and pains and assist designers in turning insights into valuable features and user experiences for a diverse population

Digital Accessibility

30 percent of the population has one or more disabilities. As we age, many of us will likely acquire disabilities. Building accessible digital products, services and societies is essential for many and beneficial to all. I am passionate about making the digital world more accessible in collaboration with disabled people of different backgrounds and ages.

This project is financed by Nordforsk as a part of the Nordic research collaboration: Infrastructures for Partially Digital Citizens: Supporting Informal Welfare Work in the Digitized State.

Wait, what is an alt-narrative?

If you're new to disability culture, you may be interested in learning more about image descriptions. Alt-text, or alternative text, is a written description, often in the form of metadata, that enables blind, low vision, and neurodivergent folks, who use screen readers, to access and enjoy digital images. Check out the project Alt-Text as Poetry or the paper Exploring AltNarrative in HCI Imagery and Comics by Makayla Lewis and colleagues (2022) to learn more about it. Typically, alt-text is hidden visually as metadata. On this site I write alt-text metadata and longer alt-narratives as captions to raise awareness about alt-text's narrative potential.

Research approach

I am committed to disability and climate justice. I am interested in creating relations of reciprocity and respect through participatory research projects. As a scholar drawing on theories from Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Anthropology, and Science and Technology Studies (STS), I advocate for community-led innovation and research driven by those most impacted by social injustice and inaccessibility.
