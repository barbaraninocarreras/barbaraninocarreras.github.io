Research approach

I am committed to disability and climate justice. I am interested in creating relations of reciprocity and respect through participatory research projects. As a scholar drawing on theories from Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Anthropology, and Science and Technology Studies (STS), I advocate for community-led innovation and research driven by those most impacted by social injustice and inaccessibility.

Current position

I am Ph.d. candidate in the Technologies in Practice research group at the IT University of Copenhagen

My project investigates how people of different backgrounds help each other to use mandatory digital self-services and infrastructures in Denmark to access welfare benefits or to communicate with the public authorities. I am interested in exploring different forms of access from the perspective of citizens. As a researcher interested in qualitative methods and design, I am committed to equity and disability justice.

This Ph.d. fellowship is financed by Nordforsk as a part of the project: Infrastructures for Partially Digital Citizens: Supporting Informal Welfare Work in the Digitized State.

  3. Teaching methods: short ethnographic studies, drawing and writing workshops, accessible design, collaborations with civil society organizations and disability activists and artists, web design, archival analysis, mapping exercises, plenum discussions, and peer review.
