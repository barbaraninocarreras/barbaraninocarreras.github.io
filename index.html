Research approach

I am committed to disability and climate justice. I am interested in creating relations of reciprocity and respect through participatory research projects. As a scholar drawing on theories from Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Anthropology, and Science and Technology Studies (STS), I advocate for community-led innovation and research driven by those most impacted by social injustice and inaccessibility.

Teaching Philosophy

  1. Core Values: reciprocity, respect, accountability
  2. Committments: multimodality, accessibility, critical inquiry and feminist intersectional pedagogy
  3. Teaching methods: short ethnographic studies, drawing and writing workshops, accessible design, collaborations with civil society organisations and disability activists and artists, web design, archival analysis, mapping exercises, plenum discussions, and peer review.
