Wait, what is an alt-narrative?

If you're new to disability culture, you may be interested in learning more about image descriptions. Alt-text, or alternative text, is a written description, often in the form of metadata, that enables blind, low vision, and neurodivergent folks, who use screen readers, to access and enjoy digital images. Check out the project Alt-Text as Poetry or the paper Exploring AltNarrative in HCI Imagery and Comics by Lewis and colleagues to learn more about it. Typically, alt-text is hidden visually as metadata. On this site I write longer alt-narratives as captions to raise awareness about alt-text's narrative potential.

Current position

I am Ph.d. candidate in the Technologies in Practice research group at the IT University of Copenhagen. My project explores how individuals from diverse backgrounds assist each other in navigating mandatory digital self-services and infrastructures in Denmark to access government benefits or communicate with the public authorities.

My research approach is intersectional. I am interested in amplifying and centering the lived experiences and knowledge of poor, queer, migrant, racialized and disabled folks most affected by inaccessible design and innovation. According to the Danish legal think tank Justitia, 20 per cent of the population is underserved by mandatory digitalization in Denmark. As the authorities acknowledge, digital services today privilige resourceful and non-disabled individuals and further marginalize poor, migrant, isolated, aging, and disabled populations. This situation calls for better policies and design approaches!

Collective Access in welfare provision

I am interested in exploring different forms of collective access in digitalized versions of provision. Collective forms of access emerge in relations of solidarity and mutual respect. In the context of welfare provision, I observe a kind of collective access politics that emerges as a response to inaccessible digital services and power assymetries between citizens and the state. My research is intended to inform digital policies and design approaches in the Nordic region so that the digitalization of welfare provision benefits minoritized and marginalized populations.

This project is financed by Nordforsk as a part of the Nordic research collaboration: Infrastructures for Partially Digital Citizens: Supporting Informal Welfare Work in the Digitized State.

Research approach

I am committed to disability and climate justice. I am interested in creating relations of reciprocity and respect through participatory research projects. As a scholar drawing on theories from Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Anthropology, and Science and Technology Studies (STS), I advocate for community-led innovation and research driven by those most impacted by social injustice and inaccessibility.
